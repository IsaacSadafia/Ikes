Solar Energy and Photovoltaic Systems

Solar energy harnesses the sun's power to generate electricity or heat.
Photovoltaic (PV) systems convert sunlight into electricity using semiconducting materials.

see fig: 2.1

Key Components.

1. Solar Panels: Convert sunlight into DC electricity.
2. Inverter: Converts DC electricity to AC for grid connection or use.
3. Mounting System: Secures solar panels to rooftops or ground.

see fig: 2.2

Benefits

1. Renewable Energy Source: Reduces dependence on fossil fuels.
2. Zero Emissions: No greenhouse gas emissions or pollution.
3. Energy Independence: Can provide power during grid outages.

Applications

1. Residential: Homes and apartments.
2. Commercial: Businesses and industries.
3. Utility-Scale: Large solar farms for grid supply.
