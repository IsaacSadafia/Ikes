Energy storage.

1.Definition: Storing energy for later use to balance supply and demand.

2. Types: - Mechanical (pumped hydro, compressed air).
- Chemical (batteries).
- Thermal (molten salt).
- Electrical (capacitors).

3. Benefits: - Improves grid stability.
- Enhances renewable energy integration.
- Reduces peak demand.

Grid Management.

1. Definition: Coordinating energy supply and demand in real-time. 2. Objectives:
- Reliability.
- Efficiency.
- Sustainability.
3. Technologies: - Advanced weather forecasting.
- Predictive analytics.
- IoT sensors and automation.
- Smart grid infrastructure.

Energy Storage and Grid Management Intersection.

1. Optimized Energy Storage: Using data analytics and forecasting to optimize energy storage deployment and operation.
2. Grid Services: Providing grid services like frequency regulation, voltage support, and spinning reserve.
3. Renewable Energy Integration: Enabling greater integration of renewable energy sources by storing excess energy for later use.

Benefits of Integrated Energy Storage and Grid Management.

1. Improved Grid Resiliency.
2. Increased Renewable Energy Penetration.
3. Enhanced Energy Efficiency.
4. Reduced Greenhouse Gas Emissions.
