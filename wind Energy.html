Wind Energy and Turbine Design

Wind energy harnesses the power of wind to generate electricity using wind turbines.

see figure 1.10

Key Components

1. Rotor Blades: Capture wind energy and convert it into mechanical energy
2. Hub: Connects rotor blades to the main shaft
3. Generator: Converts mechanical energy into electrical energy
4. Tower: Supports the turbine and elevates it to capture stronger winds

see fig 1.20

Benefits

1. Renewable Energy Source: Reduces dependence on fossil fuels
2. Low Operating Costs: No fuel costs and minimal maintenance
3. Carbon Emissions Reduction: Helps mitigate climate change

Applications

1. Utility-Scale: Large wind farms for grid supply
2. Distributed Wind: Small-scale turbines for homes, farms, and businesses
3. Offshore Wind: Turbines installed in ocean waters for increased energy capture
