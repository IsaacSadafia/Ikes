Geothermal energy

1. Definition: Geothermal energy harnesses heat from the Earth's interior to generate electricity or provide heating and cooling.
2. Source: Heat from the Earth's core, mantle, and crust.
3. Exploration: Identifying areas with suitable geology, temperature, and fluid flow.

Heat Transfer

1. Conduction: Heat transfer through direct contact between rocks and fluids.
2. Convection: Heat transfer through fluid circulation, such as hot water or steam.
3. Radiation: Heat transfer through electromagnetic waves.

Benefits

1. Renewable: Sustainable energy source.
2. Low Emissions: Minimal greenhouse gas emissions.
3. Reliability: Baseload power generation.

Challenges

1. Location-Specific: Limited to areas with suitable geology.
2. High Upfront Costs: Initial investment costs for exploration and development.
